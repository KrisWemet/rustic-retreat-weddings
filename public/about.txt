THE BEGINNING (2006)
---------------------
In 2006, Wilf and Patsy Ouimet bought 65 acres near Lac La Nonne with a dream of creating something self-sufficient - an off-grid homestead with pasture, forest, and everything they needed to live on their own terms. No power lines. No city noise. Just land that felt like theirs.

That first year, they hosted a small gathering. Family and friends came out with RVs, sat around the campfire, and soaked in the quiet. At the end of the weekend, someone said, "We should do this every year."

So they did.

What started as a single weekend became an annual tradition. Some years they'd have 80 people camping across the property - kids running wild during the day, adults around the campfire at night with music, impromptu dancing, laughter that carried through the trees. Potlucks, beer pong, cornhole, giant Jenga. Always too much food, always not enough time.


THE TURNING POINT (2018–2019)
------------------------------
In 2018, Wilf built the first part of the gazebo. After finishing it, he turned to Shannon and said, "Looks nice. Good place to get married."

He didn't know how right he'd be.

Wilf's son (also named Chris) got married on the property that same year. Then Chris and Shannon got married in 2019. After the wedding weekend, they kept hearing: "Best wedding ever." Guests told stories about moments they'd missed - the connections made, the laughter shared, the way time seemed to stop for three days.

The seed wasn't just planted anymore. It had taken root.


BUILDING THE DREAM (2019–2025)
--------------------------------
After their wedding, Shannon and Chris moved to the property full-time. The idea of opening the land as a venue shifted from "someday maybe" to "we could actually do this."

Big plans followed - a reception barn, developed campsites. Then COVID hit. Wood prices skyrocketed. What was feasible became impossible overnight.

But they didn't stop. They adapted.

Instead of one massive project, they did dozens of smaller ones. Renovated the cabin. Extended the gazebo. Started manicuring parts of the land while keeping the wild beauty intact. Spent 2020 adjusting to off-grid life, living in an RV year-round. Built a permanent house in 2023. Started renting the cabin to families in 2024 to test the waters.

Then in 2025, they opened to weddings.


WHAT THEY LEARNED (AND KEEP LEARNING)
---------------------------------------
Each wedding taught them something. Each one showed what was missing. So they built an outdoor dance floor. Added modern picnic tables and rustic ceremony benches. Kept expanding the Décor Collection.

Projects still in progress: a wash house, a bridal suite, a dedicated decor building, flush washrooms.

In 2024, a wildfire traveled through the west side of the property - stayed low, cleared undergrowth, but didn't climb the trees. New growth is filling back in. The land heals itself. They're not trying to tame it. They're trying to work with it.


THEIR PHILOSOPHY
-----------------
Most venues charge $10,000+ for a single day and lock couples into mandatory minimums - their caterer, their bar staff, their timeline.

Rustic Retreat doesn't do that.

The goal: couples build their day their way. Bring vendors for every detail and go extravagant? Great. DIY all the decor and do a potluck BBQ? Also great. They don't care how couples spend their money. They care that couples get to choose.

Their business goal: be fully booked during the four-month season with one wedding per week, every year, with very little advertising. Word of mouth. Real stories from couples who couldn't stop talking about it afterward.


THE TEAM
---------
Shannon (Chris's wife):
The heart of Rustic Retreat. Guides couples from first message to final details with calm confidence and real care. Handles communication, scheduling, and the day-to-day rhythm of the venue. When nerves show up, she's the voice that brings you back to what matters.

Chris (Shannon's husband, Wilf and Patsy's son):
The steady hand behind the scenes. Keeps the land and logistics running - power management (solar + generator), setup, and all the practical details that make the weekend feel effortless. Builds, moves, fixes, and adapts fast.

Wilf / Dad:
Handles firewood refills, built the gazebo almost entirely on his own. Works on the bigger projects - the dance floor framing, currently building the wash house.

Patsy / Mom:
Takes care of cleaning and resetting the cabin, maintaining the outhouses, and all the gardening around the property. Pitches in wherever needed.

"We're all invested. We all care. This isn't a side hustle or a passive income stream. This is our life."


WHAT MAKES THE LAND SPECIAL
-----------------------------
The moment you turn down the winding driveway and the trees close in around you, something shifts. The smell of nature. Birds singing. Sun filtering through branches.

65 acres of mixed open meadow and forest. Trails winding between trees. Natural beauty around every turn. Time slows down here.

Completely off-grid: No power lines on the property. Everything runs on solar - Wilf's original vision of self-sufficiency made real. Not a gimmick. Just how they live.
